Bohol Island

About Bohol

Bohol, located in the Central Visayas region, is one of the most beautiful islands in the Philippines. It is famous for its Chocolate Hills, white sand beaches, and the adorable Philippine tarsiers — one of the smallest primates in the world. Bohol combines natural wonders, rich history, and a vibrant local culture that makes it a must-visit destination.

Fun Facts

  • The Chocolate Hills turn brown during the dry season, resembling rows of chocolate mounds.
  • Bohol is home to the endangered Philippine tarsier, found in the Tarsier Sanctuary in Corella.
  • The Blood Compact Shrine in Tagbilaran commemorates the first treaty of friendship between Filipinos and Spaniards.

Transportation

Tricycle

Tricycles are a common mode of transport in towns like Tagbilaran and Panglao for short trips.

Ferry

Ferries connect Bohol to Cebu, Siquijor, and other nearby islands.

  • By Air: Fly directly to Bohol–Panglao International Airport from Manila, Cebu, or Davao.
  • By Sea: Fast ferries operate between Cebu City and Tagbilaran Port daily.
  • Local Transport: Tricycles, jeepneys, vans, and motorbikes are common for exploring the island.

Tourism and Development

Bohol is a model of eco-tourism in the Philippines, balancing tourism growth with environmental protection. The local government emphasizes sustainable practices, protecting marine life and promoting heritage preservation.

  • Conservation of coral reefs and mangroves through local marine sanctuaries.
  • Community-based tourism programs that empower locals.
  • Eco-friendly resorts and waste management initiatives in Panglao Island.

Culture

  • Boholanos are known for their friendliness, hospitality, and faith.
  • The Sandugo Festival celebrates the Blood Compact between Datu Sikatuna and Miguel López de Legazpi.
  • Traditional crafts like weaving and basket-making thrive in many local communities.

Climate

  • Tropical climate all year round with warm and humid conditions.
  • Best time to visit: November to May (dry season).
  • Rainy season: June to October with occasional typhoons.

Food and Cuisine

Popular Dishes in Bohol

Peanut Kisses

Iconic Bohol delicacy made from peanuts and egg whites, shaped like the Chocolate Hills.

Humba

A sweet and savory braised pork dish, a Boholano version of adobo.

Binagol

A native delicacy made from taro root, coconut milk, and brown sugar, served in coconut shells.

Grilled Squid

Freshly caught squid grilled to perfection, served on the beaches of Panglao.

Things To Do

Top Activities in Bohol

Visit the Chocolate Hills

Marvel at over 1,200 limestone hills that turn brown during the dry season.

See the Tarsiers

Visit the Philippine Tarsier Sanctuary and observe these tiny primates in their natural habitat.

Loboc River Cruise

Enjoy a scenic river cruise with buffet lunch and local music performances.

Relax on Panglao Beach

Swim, snorkel, or dive in Bohol’s most popular beach with clear waters and coral reefs.
