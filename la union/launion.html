La Union

About La Union

La Union, located in Northern Luzon, is known as the “Surfing Capital of the North.” It’s a favorite weekend getaway for city dwellers and beach lovers, offering a mix of surfing spots, scenic coastlines, delicious food, and laid-back provincial charm. Aside from the beaches, you’ll find grape farms, waterfalls, and cultural landmarks to explore.

Fun Facts

  • La Union is nicknamed “Elyu” by locals and frequent travelers.
  • It’s one of the best surfing spots in the Philippines, especially in San Juan.
  • The province also produces local wines made from fresh grapes harvested from Agoo and Bauang farms.

Transportation

Bus

Direct buses from Manila to San Fernando or San Juan take around 5 to 7 hours, depending on traffic.

Tricycle

Tricycles are a popular way to get around La Union’s towns and beaches. They’re affordable, convenient, and great for short-distance rides within San Juan and nearby areas.

  • By Bus: Victory Liner, Partas, and Viron Transit offer daily trips from Manila to La Union.
  • By Car: Approximately 5–6 hours drive from Metro Manila via NLEX, SCTEX, and TPLEX.
  • Local Transport: Tricycles and jeepneys are available for short rides between beaches, towns, and nearby attractions.

Tourism and Development

    La Union has become one of the top tourist destinations in Northern Luzon thanks to its booming surf scene, trendy cafés, and eco-friendly resorts. Local government initiatives focus on sustainable tourism, waste management, and preserving marine life to maintain its natural beauty.

  • San Juan is the heart of surfing and nightlife.
  • Bauang is known for grape farms and beach resorts.
  • San Gabriel offers eco-tourism spots like Tangadan Falls.

Culture

  • Locals are known for their warm hospitality and simplicity of life by the sea.
  • The people celebrate festivals like the Sillag Festival, highlighting light shows and coastal culture.
  • Surfing, art, and coffee culture are deeply ingrained in La Union’s identity.

Climate

  • La Union has a tropical climate with dry (November–May) and wet (June–October) seasons.
  • The best time to visit is from December to March when waves are ideal for surfing.
  • Expect warm, sunny weather most of the year, perfect for beach activities.

Food

Popular Dishes and Cafés in La Union

El Union Coffee

A popular café in San Juan famous for its iced lattes, dirty horchata, and cozy beach vibe — a must-visit for coffee lovers.

Halo-Halo de Iloko

One of La Union’s most iconic restaurants serving the classic halo-halo and local Ilocano specialties like bagnet and longganisa.

Ilocano Bagnet

Deep-fried crispy pork belly, crunchy on the outside and tender inside, often paired with tomatoes or KBL (kamatis, bagoong, lasona).

Fresh Grape Products

Locally produced grape wine, jam, and juice are unique specialties found in Bauang’s grape farms.

Things To Do

Top Activities in La Union

Surfing in San Juan

Catch waves at San Juan’s surf spots, perfect for both beginners and pros, with surf schools and gear rentals available.

Visit Tangadan Falls

Take a refreshing dip at Tangadan Falls in San Gabriel, one of La Union’s most scenic waterfalls surrounded by lush greenery.

Grape Picking in Bauang

Visit local grape farms and experience fruit picking — a fun and unique activity for families and tourists.

Watch the Sunset

Relax by the beach and witness the breathtaking sunset along San Juan’s coast — a perfect way to end your day in La Union.
