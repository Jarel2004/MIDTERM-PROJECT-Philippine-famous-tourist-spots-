Coron

About Coron

Coron, located in northern Palawan, is known for its breathtaking limestone cliffs, turquoise lagoons, and crystal-clear lakes. The island is famous for its world-class diving spots, stunning beaches, and tranquil island atmosphere, making it one of the top tourist destinations in the Philippines.

Fun Facts

  • Coron is home to several World War II Japanese shipwrecks, popular among divers worldwide.
  • Kayangan Lake is considered one of the cleanest lakes in Asia.
  • It is part of the Calamian Islands in northern Palawan.

Transportation

Tricycle

The main local transportation in Coron Town, perfect for short rides and sightseeing.

Boat

Island-hopping tours use traditional boats to take visitors to Coron’s famous lagoons and beaches.

  • By Air: Fly from Manila, Cebu, or Clark to Busuanga (Francisco B. Reyes Airport).
  • From the Airport: Take a 30-minute van ride to Coron Town.
  • Local Transport: Use tricycles, motorcycles, or boats for travel within the islands.

Tourism and Development

    Coron has gained international recognition for its natural beauty and diving attractions. The local government focuses on sustainable tourism to protect the environment while supporting the community. Strict eco-tourism policies ensure the preservation of marine life and coral reefs.

  • Limiting the number of tourists in protected areas like Kayangan Lake.
  • Implementing eco-friendly waste management and tour regulations.
  • Encouraging local communities to participate in sustainable tourism programs.

Culture

  • Coron is home to the Tagbanua people, one of the oldest ethnic groups in the Philippines.
  • The locals are known for their hospitality and deep respect for nature.
  • The Tagbanua preserve their traditions through fishing, crafts, and storytelling.

Climate

  • Tropical climate all year round.
  • Best time to visit: November to May (dry season), when the weather is sunny and calm.
  • June to October is the rainy season, with occasional storms.

Food

Popular Dishes in Coron

Grilled Fish

Freshly caught fish grilled and served with rice and dipping sauce — a Coron favorite.

Lato (Sea Grapes)

A refreshing local seaweed salad served with vinegar and tomatoes.

Cashew Nuts

Palawan is famous for its locally grown cashews, sold roasted or made into sweets.

Kinilaw

A Filipino-style ceviche made with fresh fish marinated in vinegar and spices.

Things To Do

Top Activities in Coron

Kayangan Lake

Swim in one of the cleanest lakes in Asia surrounded by stunning limestone cliffs.

Watch the Sunset at Mt. Tapyas

Climb 700 steps to enjoy a breathtaking panoramic sunset view of Coron Town.

Wreck Diving

Explore historic World War II shipwrecks beneath the turquoise waters of Coron Bay.

Relax at Maquinit Hot Spring

Enjoy a natural saltwater hot spring surrounded by mangroves, perfect after island hopping.
