Siargao Island

About Siargao Island

Siargao Island, known as the Surfing Capital of the Philippines, is a tear-shaped paradise in Surigao del Norte famous for its world-class waves, crystal-clear lagoons, and laid-back island vibe. Beyond surfing at Cloud 9, visitors can explore Sugba Lagoon, Magpupungko Rock Pools, and stunning islets like Daku, Guyam, and Naked Island—making it one of the country’s top tropical getaways for nature, adventure, and relaxation.

Fun Facts

  • Siargao is called the “Surfing Capital of the Philippines” because of its world-famous Cloud 9 surf break.
  • The island is home to one of the largest mangrove forests in Mindanao, located in the town of Del Carmen.
  • Despite its fame, Siargao still keeps a chill, small-town vibe, where most people get around on motorbikes and everyone knows everyone.

Transportation

Motorbike (Habal-Habal)

The most popular and convenient way to explore Siargao’s beaches, lagoons, and scenic roads.

Tricycle

Commonly used for short trips around towns like General Luna and Dapa, perfect for quick and affordable rides.

  • By air: Siargao has its own airport in Del Carmen, with direct flights from Manila, Cebu, and Davao.
  • To famous places: Visitors usually ride vans or habal-habal (motorbike taxis) to reach popular spots like Cloud 9, Sugba Lagoon, and Magpupungko Pools.
  • Local Transport: The most common local transportation is the motorbike, which can be rented easily for exploring the island’s scenic roads.

Tourism and Development

    Siargao’s tourism has rapidly grown from a quiet surf haven into one of the top island destinations in the Philippines, attracting travelers worldwide for its waves, lagoons, and laid-back charm. This growth has boosted the local economy, creating jobs and supporting eco-tourism initiatives, while also encouraging sustainable practices to protect its beaches, mangroves, and marine life.

  • Recognized globally as the Surfing Capital of the Philippines.
  • Promotes eco-tourism and sustainable travel.
  • Drives local business growth in resorts, tours, and dining.

Culture

    Siargao’s culture is a vibrant blend of Surigaonon traditions, island hospitality, and a growing surf community that embraces simplicity and respect for nature. Locals are known for their warmth, friendliness, and close community ties, often celebrating life through music, festivals, and shared meals.

  • Deeply rooted in Surigaonon and fishing traditions.
  • Strong sense of community and island hospitality.
  • Influenced by the surf culture that promotes unity and simplicity.

Climate

    Siargao Island experiences a tropical climate characterized by warm temperatures, high humidity, and two main seasons — the dry season (March to October) and the wet season (November to February). The dry season brings sunny weather ideal for travel and island activities, while the wet season attracts surfers with powerful Pacific swells. Temperatures range from 25°C to 32°C, and the island’s constant sea breeze keeps it pleasant throughout the year.

  • Two seasons: Dry (Mar–Oct) and Wet (Nov–Feb)
  • Ideal surfing conditions during the wet season
  • Warm temperatures year-round (25°C–32°C)

Food

Popular Filipino Dishes

Pan de Surf

A local bread shaped like a surfboard, often enjoyed with coffee or as a snack.

Kinilaw

Siargao’s version of ceviche made with fresh fish marinated in vinegar, calamansi, and spices.

Adobo sa Gata

A creamy twist on the classic adobo, cooked with coconut milk for a rich, island flavor.

Seafood Platter

A mix of freshly caught fish, crabs, and shrimps grilled or cooked in coconut-based sauces.

Things To Do

Top Activities in Siargao Island

Surf at Cloud 9

Experience world-class waves at the famous surf spot, perfect for both beginners and pros.

Island Hopping

Visit nearby gems like Daku Island, Guyam Island, and Naked Island for swimming and snorkeling.

Explore the Sugba Lagoon

Paddleboard or swim in this stunning turquoise lagoon surrounded by lush mangroves.

Visit the Magpupungko Rock Pools

Take a dip in crystal-clear natural tidal pools that appear during low tide.
