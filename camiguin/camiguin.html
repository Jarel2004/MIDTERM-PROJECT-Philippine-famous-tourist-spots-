Camiguin Island

About Camiguin Island

Camiguin Island, known as the “Island Born of Fire,” is a small yet breathtaking province in Northern Mindanao famous for its seven volcanoes, lush landscapes, and crystal-clear waters. It offers a perfect mix of adventure and relaxation, from exploring the stunning White Island sandbar and snorkeling at the historic Sunken Cemetery to trekking Mt. Hibok-Hibok and cooling off at Katibawasan Falls. Rich in culture and natural beauty, Camiguin also delights visitors with its sweet lanzones fruit and warm local hospitality, making it one of the most enchanting and peaceful destinations in the Philippines.

Fun Facts

  • Camiguin has more volcanoes (7) than towns, earning it its fiery nickname.
  • Its famous White Island sandbar changes shape and size depending on the tide.
  • The island is known for its sweet lanzones fruit and celebrates the Lanzones Festival every October.

Transportation

Motorcycles (Habal-habal)

The most common way to get around the island, offering flexibility for exploring narrow roads and scenic routes.

Multicabs

Small shared vehicles used for local transport between towns and tourist spots.

  • By air: Travelers can reach Camiguin via Camiguin Airport in Mambajao, with regular flights from Cebu and connecting routes from Manila.
  • To famous places: Vans and multicabs are available for tours to popular attractions like White Island, Katibawasan Falls, and the Sunken Cemetery.
  • Local Transport: Motorcycles or habal-habal are the most common local transport, ideal for short trips and exploring hidden spots around the island.

Tourism and Development

    Tourism in Camiguin Island has become a major driver of local growth, blending natural beauty with cultural heritage to attract both local and international visitors. The island’s pristine beaches, waterfalls, and volcanoes promote eco-tourism, while government and community efforts focus on preserving its environment and traditions. Development projects aim to improve infrastructure and accessibility without compromising the island’s peaceful and natural charm.

  • Promotes eco-friendly and sustainable tourism.
  • Provides jobs and income for local communities.
  • Encourages environmental protection and cultural preservation.

Culture

    Camiguin’s culture blends indigenous roots, Spanish influence, and strong Catholic faith, reflected in its lively festivals, traditions, and warm hospitality. The famous Lanzones Festival celebrates gratitude, unity, and the island’s rich harvest.

  • Celebrates the Lanzones Festival with dance and thanksgiving.
  • Deep Catholic influence shapes daily life and traditions.
  • Known for its hospitality and community spirit.

Climate

    Camiguin Island has a tropical climate with warm weather all year round, marked by a dry season from March to June and a wet season from July to February. The island enjoys plenty of sunshine, making it ideal for outdoor adventures and beach activities.

  • Warm and humid climate year-round.
  • Rainy season from July to February.
  • Best time to visit is during the dry months (March–June).

Food

Popular Filipino Dishes

Lanzones

The island’s signature sweet fruit, celebrated every October during the Lanzones Festival.

Kiping

A colorful, crispy delicacy made from ground cassava, often served during festivals.

Pastel

A soft, sweet bun filled with yema (custard), a famous Camiguin pasalubong loved by visitors.

Surol

A hearty, sour beef soup made with innards and bile, commonly enjoyed by locals as comfort food.

Things To Do

Top Activities in Camiguin Island

Visit White Island Sandbar

Relax and enjoy stunning views of Mt. Hibok-Hibok and the sea.

Explore the Sunken Cemetery

Snorkel or dive around this historic underwater landmark.

Trek Mt. Hibok-Hibok

Hike the island’s active volcano for adventure and panoramic views.

Swim at Katibawasan Falls

Cool off in the refreshing waters beneath a 70-meter waterfall.
