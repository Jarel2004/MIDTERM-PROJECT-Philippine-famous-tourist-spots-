Batanes Island

About Batanes Island

Escape to the northernmost part of the Philippines, where postcard-perfect views and awe-inspiring landscapes await - Batanes Island. So secluded and far from the main islands of the country, Batanes is actually closer to Taiwan than to Manila City, the country capital. As the smallest province in the Philippines, Batanes Island offers a peaceful and unspoiled haven, where time seems to slow down, making it the perfect destination for those looking to unwind and explore untouched beauty.

Fun Facts

  • The Batanes Islands are included by UNESCO in the list of candidates for the World Heritage List.
  • This province of the Philippines has practically "zero" crime rate.
  • According to country's official statistics Batanes rank number 6 out of 82 Philippines'provinces by the overall Human Development Index.

Transportation

Tricycle

Tricycle is most common transport on Batanes islands.

Faluwa

If your Batanes itinerary includes a trip to nearby islands like Sabtang or Itbayat, you'll need to take a traditional Ivatan passenger boat known as a faluwa.

  • By Air: The only way to reach Batanes is by air. Regular flights are available from Manila, Clark, and occasionally from Cebu to Basco Airport, the main gateway to the province.
  • From Basco Airport: Tricycles are the main mode of transport around town, and vans can be rented for island tours.
  • Local Transport: Travelers can explore Batanes by tricycle, bicycle, or van, enjoying scenic views along well-paved coastal roads.

Tourism and Development

    Batanes is known for its unspoiled beauty, rolling hills, and unique stone houses that attract both local and foreign travelers. Tourism development here is centered on sustainability and preserving the Ivatan way of life.

  • Top attractions include Marlboro Hills, Basco Lighthouse, and Sabtang Island.
  • Eco-friendly accommodations and guided cultural tours promote responsible tourism.
  • The local government ensures strict preservation of nature and heritage sites.

Culture

  • The locals, known as Ivatans, are admired for their honesty, resilience, and deep sense of community.
  • Their stone houses, built to withstand strong typhoons, reflect their adaptability and craftsmanship.
  • Batanes culture emphasizes simplicity, cooperation, and harmony with nature.

Climate

  • Batanes has a cooler climate compared to most of the Philippines due to its northern location.
  • Best time to visit: March to June, when the weather is calm and skies are clear.
  • The province experiences strong winds and occasional typhoons from July to December.

Food

Traditional Ivatan Dishes

Luñis

A traditional Ivatan dish similar to adobo but cooked without soy sauce. Pork is slowly simmered in its own fat and salt until golden brown and tender. Best enjoyed with garlic rice and turmeric (yellow) rice.

Uvud Balls

A savory dish made from finely grated banana pith, mixed with minced meat or fish and shaped into balls. It showcases the Ivatans’ resourcefulness in using local ingredients.

Vunes

A dish made of dried taro stalks that are soaked, cleaned, and sautéed with pork and garlic. It’s a traditional way of preserving vegetables and reflects the Ivatans’ sustainable lifestyle.

Payi (Lobster)

Fresh lobsters, locally known as “payi,” are among Batanes’ seafood specialties. They’re often grilled or steamed to highlight their natural sweetness and freshness from the surrounding seas.

Things To Do

Top Activities in Batanes

Visit Marlboro Hills

Marvel at the breathtaking rolling hills and endless views of the sea and sky. This spot offers one of the best panoramic views in Batanes, often compared to the landscapes of New Zealand.

Drop by the Honesty Coffee Shop

Experience the Ivatan people’s honesty and integrity at this iconic unmanned store where customers pay for what they take — no staff, just trust.

See the Chamantad–Tinyan Viewpoint

Take in one of the most scenic views in Batanes, with dramatic cliffs, rolling hills, and turquoise waters. Perfect for photography and relaxation.

Explore the Ivatan Stone Houses

Walk through the traditional village of Savidug to see centuries-old stone houses built to withstand the strong Batanes typhoons.
