Batanes Island

About Boracay

Escape to the northernmost part of the Philippines, where postcard-perfect views and awe-inspiring landscapes await - Batanes Island. So secluded and far from the main islands of the country, Batanes is actually closer to Taiwan than to Manila City, the country capital. As the smallest province in the Philippines, Batanes Island offers a peaceful and unspoiled haven, where time seems to slow down, making it the perfect destination for those looking to unwind and explore untouched beauty.

Fun Facts

  • The Batanes Islands are included by UNESCO in the list of candidates for the World Heritage List.
  • This province of the Philippines has practically "zero" crime rate.
  • According to country's official statistics Batanes rank number 6 out of 82 Philippines'provinces by the overall Human Development Index.

Transportation

E-Bike

Eco-friendly and quiet, e-bikes are common way to explore Boracay's narrow roads.

Tricycle

The most popular mode of transportation on the island, perfect for short rides between beaches.

  • By Air: Fly from Manila or Cebu to either Caticlan or Kalibo Airport.
  • From Caticlan: Take a short tricycle ride to the jetty port, then ride a boat to Boracay.
  • Local Transport: On the island, you can get around by tricycle, e-bike or by walking along the beach path.

Tourism and Development

    Boracay was temporarily closed for rehabilitation in 2018 because of the environmental issues such as pollution and overcrowding. After a six-month closure, the island reopened with stricter environmental regulations, including:

  • Limiting the number of tourists allowed on the island.
  • Implementing a strict no-smoking policy on the beach.
  • Rehabilitating the beach and surrounding areas to restore their natural beauty.

Culture

  • Locals are called Aklanon (from the province of Aklan).
  • The local language is Aklanon, but Tagalog and English are widely spoken.
  • The people are known for being friendly and hospitable,expecially to tourists.

Climate

  • Tropical climate all year round
  • Best time to visit:November to May (dry season), when the weather is sunny and the sea is calm.
  • June to October is rainy season, with occasional typhoons.
