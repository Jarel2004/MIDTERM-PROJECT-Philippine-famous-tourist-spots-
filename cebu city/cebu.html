Cebu City

About Cebu City

Cebu City, known as the “Queen City of the South,” is a major center of commerce, culture, history, and education in the Philippines. It blends modern urban life with historical heritage, beautiful beaches on nearby islands, and vibrant local life.

Fun Facts

  • Cebu was the first Spanish settlement and capital of the Philippines.
  • The famous Magellan’s Cross marks the spot where Ferdinand Magellan planted a cross in 1521.
  • Santo Niño de Cebu is one of the oldest religious relics in the country, enshrined in the Basilica del Santo Niño.

Transportation

Jeepney

Jeepneys are a common and cheap way to get around the city and its suburbs.

Ferry

Ferries depart from Cebu City to nearby islands like Mactan, Bantayan, and Malapascua.

  • By Air: Cebu–Mactan International Airport connects Cebu City with domestic and international flights.
  • By Sea: Cebu City has a major port with ferries to Luzon, Mindanao, and Visayas islands.
  • Local Transport: Use jeepneys, taxis, motorcycles, and ride-hailing services for moving around.

Tourism and Development

Cebu City is a hub of tourism and development. It preserves heritage sites while growing business districts, shopping centers, and resorts. Efforts are being made to improve infrastructure, reduce traffic congestion, and maintain environmental quality.

  • Restoration and preservation of historical sites (e.g. Fort San Pedro, Magellan’s Cross).
  • Urban greening projects and city beautification.
  • Sustainable tourism promotion on nearby beaches and islands to relieve city crowding.

Culture

  • Cebuanos are known for their resilience, warmth, hospitality, and strong religious traditions.
  • The Sinulog Festival, held every January, is one of the Philippines’ biggest festivals celebrating the Santo Niño.
  • Arts, crafts, and local cuisine reflect influences from Cebu’s history of trade, colonization, and modern growth.

Climate

  • Tropical climate, warm to hot throughout the year.
  • Best time to visit: December to May when rainfall is lower.
  • Rainy season: June to November, with possible typhoons and heavy rains.

Food and Cuisine

Popular Dishes in Cebu City

Lechon Cebu

Cebu’s roasted pig, crispy skin and flavorful meat, often called the best lechon in the Philippines.

SUTUKIL

A style of cooking: **Su**ba (grill), **Tu**log (stew), **Kil**aw (ceviche) mainly with fresh seafood.

Danggit

Dried fish, often fried and served with rice and pickled mangoes or vinegar dip.

Puso

Rice cooked inside woven coconut leaf pouches — staple accompaniment for many Cebu meals.

Things To Do

Top Activities in Cebu City

Visit Magellan’s Cross

See the iconic cross planted by Ferdinand Magellan in 1521, located in downtown Cebu.

Fort San Pedro

The oldest Spanish fort in the Philippines, offering history and ocean views.

Tops Lookout

Climb or drive up to Tops for panoramic views of Cebu City, especially at sunset.

Swim with Whale Sharks at Oslob

Take a day trip to Oslob to see whale sharks up close in their natural environment.
