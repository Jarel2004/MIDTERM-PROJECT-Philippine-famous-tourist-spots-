El Nido, Palawan

About El Nido

El Nido, located in the northern tip of Palawan, is one of the most breathtaking destinations in the Philippines. Known for its crystal-clear waters, towering limestone cliffs, hidden lagoons, and white-sand beaches, it’s a true paradise for nature and adventure lovers.

Fun Facts

  • El Nido means “The Nest” in Spanish, named after the edible swiftlet nests found in its cliffs.
  • It’s part of the Bacuit Bay archipelago, consisting of around 45 islands and islets.
  • El Nido has been featured in numerous international travel magazines as one of the world’s most beautiful islands.

Transportation

Van

From Puerto Princesa, you can take a 5–6 hour van or bus ride to El Nido.

Boat

Island-hopping boats take visitors to explore El Nido’s lagoons and beaches daily.

  • By Air: There are direct flights to El Nido (Lio Airport) and Puerto Princesa International Airport.
  • By Land: Vans and buses connect Puerto Princesa to El Nido daily.
  • By Sea: Private boats and ferries link El Nido with Coron and other Palawan islands.

Tourism and Development

El Nido’s tourism focuses on sustainable and eco-friendly practices. The local government and resorts aim to balance tourism growth with environmental conservation, protecting marine life and coral reefs.

  • Strict eco-tourism rules to limit visitor numbers per island.
  • Use of renewable energy and waste reduction programs in resorts.
  • Community-based tourism supporting local livelihoods.

Culture

  • El Nido’s residents are mostly Cuyonon and Tagbanua, known for their hospitality and craftsmanship.
  • Local culture emphasizes respect for nature and traditional fishing practices.
  • Festivals and music reflect the peaceful island life and deep community spirit.

Climate

  • Tropical climate: warm all year with average temperatures of 26–32°C.
  • Dry season: November to May — perfect for island-hopping and diving.
  • Rainy season: June to October, with occasional storms.

Food and Cuisine

Popular Dishes in El Nido

Grilled Seafood

Fresh fish, shrimp, squid, and lobsters grilled right by the beach — a staple in El Nido dining.

Kinilaw

Fresh raw fish marinated in vinegar, lime, and spices — the Filipino version of ceviche.

Coconut-based Dishes

Local recipes made with coconut milk, giving dishes a rich tropical flavor.

Halo-Halo

A refreshing Filipino dessert made with shaved ice, sweet beans, fruits, and ice cream — perfect for island heat.

Things To Do

Explore Big Lagoon

Kayak through emerald waters surrounded by dramatic limestone cliffs.

Swim in Small Lagoon

Enter through a narrow rock opening into a peaceful lagoon ideal for snorkeling.

Relax at Nacpan Beach

Four kilometers of golden sand and clear water — one of the world’s most beautiful beaches.

Island Hopping Tours

Join Tour A, B, C, or D to discover hidden lagoons, secret beaches, and coral reefs.
