Donsol

About Donsol

Donsol is a coastal municipality in the province of Sorsogon, in the Bicol Region of Luzon, Philippines. It covers about 156.20 km² and has a population of approximately 50,281 as of the 2020 census. It’s often dubbed the “Whale Shark Capital of the Philippines”, thanks to the large numbers of gentle whale sharks (locally called butanding) that visit its waters.

Fun Facts

  • Donsol is world-famous for its gentle giant visitors, the butanding (whale sharks), which can be seen up close during peak season from November to June.
  • Instead of exploiting whale sharks, Donsol built a sustainable tourism model that protects them while providing jobs to local guides and boatmen.
  • Aside from whale sharks, Donsol is also known for its enchanting firefly river cruises at night, where thousands of glowing fireflies light up mangrove trees along the Donsol River.

Transportation

Boat (Bangka)

Used for whale shark watching tours and exploring the surrounding waters and islands.

Tricycle

The most common local transport used for getting around town and reaching nearby resorts, beaches, and tourist spots.

  • By air: The nearest airport is Legazpi Airport, about 1.5 hours away from Donsol, with flights from Manila and Cebu.
  • To famous places: Vans and tricycles are commonly used to reach tourist spots like the Whale Shark Interaction Center and nearby beaches.
  • Local Transport: Tricycles are the main local transport within the town, ideal for short distances and daily commutes.

Tourism and Development

    Donsol has grown into one of the Philippines’ top eco-tourism destinations, mainly known for its sustainable whale shark (butanding) interaction tours. The local government and environmental groups have worked together to promote responsible tourism that supports both marine conservation and community livelihood.

  • Promotes eco-friendly whale shark tourism recognized worldwide.
  • Provides employment and income for local fishermen and tour guides.
  • Encourages environmental awareness and protection of marine life.

Culture

    Donsol’s culture is deeply rooted in Bicolano traditions, blending coastal livelihood, strong family ties, and vibrant festivals. The locals are known for their warm hospitality, love for music and dance, and devotion to religious celebrations like the Our Lady of the Most Holy Rosary Festival.

  • Celebrates colorful festivals showcasing local faith and community spirit.
  • Known for Bicolano cuisine, crafts, and traditional dances.
  • Reflects a harmonious connection between people, nature, and the sea.

Climate

    Donsol has a tropical climate with warm temperatures year-round. The dry season usually lasts from December to May, ideal for whale shark watching and outdoor activities, while the rainy season from June to November brings heavier rainfall and occasional typhoons.

  • Tropical weather perfect for marine and eco-tourism.
  • Dry season offers calm seas and clear skies.
  • Rainy season sustains the lush greenery and marine life.

Food

Popular Filipino Dishes

Bicol Express

A spicy pork dish cooked in coconut milk and chili, famous across the Bicol Region.

Laing

Made from dried taro leaves simmered in coconut milk, chili, and shrimp or meat.

Kinunot na Pagi

A local specialty made from stingray or shark flakes cooked in coconut milk with malunggay leaves and chili.

Pancit Bato

A flavorful noodle dish made with local “Bato” noodles, stir-fried with vegetables, soy sauce, and meat or seafood.
