Vigan City

About Vigan City

Vigan City, the capital of Ilocos Sur and a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is famed for its well-preserved Spanish colonial architecture and cobblestone streets. Founded in 1572 by Juan de Salcedo, it blends Filipino, Chinese, and Spanish influences seen in Calle Crisologo, St. Paul’s Cathedral, and traditional Burnay pottery. Known for its longganisa, empanada, and bagnet, Vigan remains a vibrant symbol of Philippine history and culture.

Fun Facts

  • Vigan is one of Asia’s best-preserved Spanish colonial cities, famous for its cobblestone streets and ancestral houses.
  • You can explore the city’s historic streets on a horse-drawn carriage, just like people did centuries ago.
  • In 2015, Vigan was recognized as one of the New7Wonders Cities of the World for its culture and heritage.

Transportation

Calesa

A calesa is a traditional two-wheeled horse-drawn carriage in Vigan City, offering a nostalgic way to explore its historic cobblestone streets.

Tricycle

A tricycle in Vigan City is a convenient and affordable three-wheeled vehicle for short trips.

  • By air: Travelers can reach Vigan City via Laoag International Airport, about two hours away by land.
  • To famous places: Private vans or tour vehicles are commonly used to visit Vigan’s heritage sites and nearby attractions.
  • Local Transport: The calesa and tricycle are popular local rides for exploring the city’s streets and landmarks.

Tourism and Development

    Vigan City’s tourism boosts its economy by drawing visitors to its UNESCO-listed heritage sites, supporting local industries like pottery, weaving, and food, and inspiring efforts to preserve its cultural identity.

  • Promotes heritage conservation and cultural pride.
  • Provides jobs and income for local residents.
  • Supports traditional crafts and small businesses.

Culture

    Vigan City’s culture reflects a rich blend of Filipino, Spanish, and Chinese influences seen in its architecture, crafts, festivals, and cuisine, preserving traditions that date back to the colonial era.

  • Celebrated through the Viva Vigan Festival of the Arts.
  • Known for traditional abel weaving and burnay pottery.
  • Famous for local dishes like Vigan longganisa and empanada.

Climate

    Vigan City has a tropical climate with hot, dry summers from March to May and a rainy season from June to October, while the cooler months last from November to February.

  • Dry season: March to May, ideal for sightseeing.
  • Rainy season: June to October with frequent showers.
  • Cool months: November to February, best for travel.

Food

Popular Filipino Dishes

Vigan Longganisa

A small, garlicky, and tangy native sausage that’s usually fried and paired with rice and vinegar.

Vigan Empanada

A deep-fried orange pastry filled with longganisa, egg, and grated green papaya, crispy on the outside and savory inside.

Bagnet

A famous Ilocano deep-fried pork belly that’s crunchy outside and tender inside, often eaten with tomatoes or vinegar.

Sinanglao

A hearty, sour beef soup made with innards and bile, commonly enjoyed by locals as comfort food.
