Banaue Rice Terraces

About Banaue Rice Terraces

The mountainous Banaue in Ifugao province located in the northern part of Luzon has captured the interest of both local and international travelers because of its rich culture, cool weather, untouched mountain ranges and the warmth of the local Ifugao people. The Banaue Rice Terraces, often referred to as the "Eighth Wonder of the World," are a testament to the ingenuity and hard work of the Ifugao people who carved these terraces into the mountains over 2,000 years ago using traditional methods passed down through generations. These terraces are not only a marvel of engineering but also a symbol of the Ifugao's deep connection to their land and culture.

Fun Facts

  • Banaue Rice Terraces were carved into the mountains over 2,000 years ago.
  • They are often referred to as the "Eighth Wonder of the World."
  • The terraces are still used for rice cultivation today.

Transportation

Bus

Buses are a common and affordable way to travel between cities and towns in the Philippines.

Tricycle

The most popular mode of transportation on the island, perfect for short rides between beaches.

  • By Bus From Manila:

    • Traveling from Manila to Banaue (Ifugao province) is exclusively by land transportation, with no direct flights available; the trip takes approximately 9 hours, highly dependent on traffic. Most visitors prefer to travel at night to arrive early and maximize their time exploring Banaue. You have two main bus options: Ohayami Bus Transit, located at the corner of Fajardo Street and Lacson Avenue in Sampaloc, Manila, offers a daily trip leaving at 9:00 PM and allows online booking for reservations. Alternatively, Dangwa Transit, with a terminal in Cubao, operates only one trip daily departing around 8:00 PM and accommodates passengers on a first-come, first-served basis, so early arrival is essential.

  • By Bus From Baguio:

    The bus from Baguio to Banaue takes approximately 3 hours including transfers and departs at least three times a day. You can go by Ohayami Bus transit stationed along Otek Street near Burnham Park or KMS bus that offers two trips daily and can be found near Rizal Park.

  • By Bus From Sagada:

    If you’re coming from Sagada, it’s advisable to get to the terminal early. At the Sagada Municipal Hall, there are jeepneys going to Bontoc (the capital of Mountain Province). Travel time is around an hour and the trip starts as early as 6:30 a.m. There’s an available trip that leaves every half hour until 9:00 a.m to 1 p.m. From Bontoc, you can take the local bus to Banaue. The first trip leaves at 7:00 a.m and takes around 2 hours. Another option is to ride a van that leaves at 8:30 a.m., 10:00 a.m., and 1:00 p.m.

Tourism and Development

Tourism in Banaue centers on the ancient rice terraces, a UNESCO World Heritage site known as the "8th Wonder of the World," which draws visitors for scenic views and trekking. Development is a key issue, with concerns that tourism may be harming the terraces, while also being a potential engine for a more sustainable local economy and culture. Initiatives like the DOT-CAR's support for golf tourism suggest efforts to diversify and grow the industry in the region

Culture

The Ifugao People and Their Heritage

  • The main language spoken is Ifugao, but Tagalog and English are commonly used, especially in the tourism industry.
  • The Ifugao are masters of traditional wooden carvings, particularly bul-ul (rice gods), which are spiritual symbols of fertility and prosperity.
  • The community maintains a strong sense of cooperation and tradition, with ceremonies often centered around the rice cycle and ancestral worship.

Climate

The best months to visit Banaue are during the months of April to May and October to November since these are the times when the rice terraces are at their greenest and most scenic state. Visiting during the rainy seasons of July and August is not recommended due to the possibility of landslides in some areas making it difficult for activities like hiking and trekking.

Food

Where to Eat in Banaue

7th Heaven’s Café

It offers a good selection of breakfast and other comfort food while your eyes feast on the amazing views of the rice terraces. Don’t miss out on their famous honey-cured liempo and tinocenturi, their own version of the popular Filipino dish tinola.

Las Vegas Lodge

You can never go wrong with a scenic view, live music, and great food. Good thing, Las Vegas Lodge offers all of them. Known for their variety of both local and international cuisine, this place is visited by travelers who are after affordability and tasty meals.

Sanafe Lodge

Sanafe Lodge and Restaurant, located in Banaue, Ifugao, offers a cozy and safe retreat for travelers. The lodge provides indoor and outdoor seating options for guests to enjoy the stunning views of the rice terraces. Owned by Susan Paredes, this accommodation is known for its hospitable staff and comfortable ambiance. The on-site restaurant serves a variety of Filipino and international dishes, including fresh salads and grilled meats that are popular among visitors.

Banaue Homestay

Home-cooked meal at a very affordable price makes you feel like you’ve never really left home. For about PHP100 (USD 2), you already have a complete set of meal consists of the main course dishes, rice and ripe mango for dessert. Not a bad deal!
