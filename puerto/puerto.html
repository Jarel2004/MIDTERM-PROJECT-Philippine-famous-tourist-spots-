Puerto Princesa

About Puerto Princesa Underground River

The Puerto Princesa Underground River is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in the city of Puerto Princesa, Palawan, Philippines. It is known for its stunning limestone karst landscapes and the underground river that flows beneath the mountains, making it one of the most unique natural wonders in the world.

Fun Facts

  • The underground river is approximately 8.2 kilometers long and is one of the longest navigable underground rivers in the world.
  • It is located within the Puerto Princesa Subterranean River National Park, a protected area known for its rich biodiversity.
  • The river flows directly into the South China Sea, and its unique ecosystem supports various species of flora and fauna.

To visit the Puerto Princesa Subterranean River National Park, you’ll first need to make your way to Puerto Princesa City, the vibrant capital of the island province of Palawan. You can get to Puerto Princesa by land, sea, or air. The most convenient and quickest option is to book flights to Puerto Princesa City, which is easily accessible from major cities across the Philippines. Once you’ve arrived in Puerto Princesa CIty, the next step is to travel to Sabang, a small village located north of the city and the gateway to the Underground River. Head to San Jose Terminal to catch a jeepney, bus, or van to Sabang village, a journey that typically takes two to three hours.

  • By Jeepney or Bus

    • To get to Sabang village by jeepney or bus, head to the San Jose Terminal, located at the San Jose New Market in Puerto Princesa City. From the terminal, you'll find jeepneys and non-air-conditioned buses that make their way to Sabang Port. Scheduled departure times are 7:00 AM, 9:00 AM, 12:00 PM, and 2:00 PM. Jeepneys and buses typically wait until all seats are filled before departing.The journey usually takes around 1.5 to 2 hours, with travel time ranging from two to three hours, including stopovers along the way.

  • By Van

    Another option for reaching Sabang village is by van, which offers a faster, more comfortable ride. These vans make the trip in about two hours or less, providing a quicker and more direct route compared to jeepneys or buses. Upon reaching Sabang Port, you'll need to present your permit at the Tourism Office and pay the environmental and boat fees, along with the mandatory audio guide. A designated boat will then take you to the Underground River's entrance, where you'll switch to a smaller paddle boat for the cave tour.

Tourism and Development

Tourism has played a significant role in the development of Puerto Princesa and the surrounding areas, particularly with the popularity of the Underground River as a major tourist attraction. The influx of visitors has led to increased economic activity, job creation, and infrastructure development in the region. The local government has implemented various measures to ensure sustainable tourism practices, including limiting the number of daily visitors to the Underground River and promoting eco-friendly initiatives. Efforts have also been made to preserve the natural environment and protect the biodiversity of the area. Additionally, tourism has helped raise awareness about the importance of environmental conservation and cultural heritage among both locals and visitors. Overall, tourism has contributed positively to the development of Puerto Princesa while emphasizing the need for responsible and sustainable practices to preserve its natural wonders for future generations.

Culture

  • Puerto Princesa is known for its rich cultural heritage, influenced by various ethnic groups and historical events.
  • The city celebrates numerous festivals, showcasing its vibrant traditions and community spirit.
  • The local cuisine reflects the diverse cultural influences, with a focus on fresh seafood and local ingredients.
  • The arts and crafts of Puerto Princesa, including woven products and traditional music, are vital expressions of its cultural identity.

Climate

The best time to explore the Puerto Princesa Underground River is during the dry season, from November to May. This period offers calm weather and smoother waters for a more enjoyable boat ride. Visiting during the dry season also reduces the chance of cancellations or delays due to bad weather. On the other hand, the warmest months in Puerto Princesa are from March to May, while the wet season lasts from June to October. Be sure to check weather updates as your trip approaches to stay informed.

Food

Where to Eat in Puerto Princesa Underground River

Fresh Grilled Fish / Seafood

Simple, fresh, flavorful. Often you can ask them to grill what’s local.

Crocodile Sisig

Crocodile Sisig is a unique twist on the traditional Filipino dish, made with tender crocodile meat that's been marinated and grilled to perfection. It's typically served on a sizzling plate with onions, chili peppers, and a squeeze of calamansi for an extra kick.

Chao Long Noodle

Chao Long is a popular noodle dish from Puerto Princesa, known for its rich and flavorful broth made from beef or pork. It's typically served with a hard-boiled egg and a generous amount of grated cheese on top. This comforting dish is perfect for warming up after a day of exploring the beautiful rice terraces.

Kinilaw

Kinilaw is a traditional Filipino dish made from fresh raw fish, typically tuna or mackerel, marinated in vinegar and citrus juices. It's often mixed with onions, ginger, and chili peppers for added flavor. This refreshing dish is perfect for hot days and is a must-try when visiting Puerto Princesa.

Nearby Attractions and Activities

Sabang X-Zipline

For an adrenaline rush with stunning views, don’t miss the Sabang X-Zipline. The adventure begins with a scenic 30-minute trek through the mangroves, leading you up a small mountain to the zipline launch point. From there, you’ll soar over the lush forest canopy and the beautiful Sabang Beach, taking in breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape. The zipline ride itself is exhilarating, offering a unique perspective of the area as you glide through the air. It’s an unforgettable experience that combines adventure with the natural beauty of Puerto Princesa.

Ugong Rock Adventures

Ugong Rock is one of the top adventure tours in the Philippines and makes a perfect complement to the Underground River tour. This stunning limestone formation rises 75 feet high and offers a thrilling mix of activities for all types of adventurers.

Monkey Trail

For a more immersive experience, consider hiking back to Sabang via the 5-kilometer Monkey Trail (also known as the Jungle Trail) after your Underground River tour. This scenic trail takes you through lush forests, towering limestone cliffs, and diverse wildlife.

Sabang Mangrove Paddle Boat Tour

For a peaceful yet thrilling side trip to your Underground River tour, consider the Sabang Mangrove Paddle Boat Tour. As you glide through the tranquil waters, you'll be surrounded by lush mangrove forests that play a key role in preserving the unique ecosystem of Palawan Island.
