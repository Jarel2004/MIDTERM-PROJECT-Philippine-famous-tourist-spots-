Tagaytay City

About Tagaytay

Tagaytay is a popular holiday town south of Manila on the Philippine island Luzon. Known for its mild climate, it sits on a ridge above Taal Volcano Island, an active surrounded by Taal Lake. Overlooking the area, People's Park in the Sky occupies the grounds of a never-finished presidential mansion. Picnic Grove is a recreation are with trails and a zip line.

Fun Facts

  • Legendarily, the word Tagaytay came from "TAGA" meaning to cut and "ITAY" which means father.
  • It is now classified as a Second Class City as declared by the Department of Finance.
  • Although Taal Volcano is readily seen from the ridge of Tagaytay City, it is actually in a different province!

Transportation

Jeepney

The jeepney is a staple mode of transportation in the Philippines, and it's a great way to travel arouns Tagaytay City without breaking the bank.

Tricycle

If you're looking for a quicker way to reach the city's must-see spots, hopping on a tricycle is your go-to option.

/*
  • By Air: Fly from Manila or Cebu to either Caticlan or Kalibo Airport.
  • From Caticlan: Take a short tricycle ride to the jetty port, then ride a boat to Boracay.
  • Local Transport: On the island, you can get around by tricycle, e-bike or by walking along the beach path.

Tourism and Development

    Boracay was temporarily closed for rehabilitation in 2018 because of the environmental issues such as pollution and overcrowding. After a six-month closure, the island reopened with stricter environmental regulations, including:

  • Limiting the number of tourists allowed on the island.
  • Implementing a strict no-smoking policy on the beach.
  • Rehabilitating the beach and surrounding areas to restore their natural beauty.

Culture

  • Locals are called Aklanon (from the province of Aklan).
  • The local language is Aklanon, but Tagalog and English are widely spoken.
  • The people are known for being friendly and hospitable,expecially to tourists.

Climate

  • Tropical climate with two distinct season.
  • Best time to visit: During the summer months from March to May, the temperature rarely exceeds 30°C (86°F), making it a comfortable escape from the heat of the lowlands. The coolest time of year is typically in February, with an average temperature of 18.8°C (65.8°F).

Food

Popular Filipino Dishes

Adobo

Chicken or pork braised in soy sauce, vinegar, and garlic — the classic Filipino favorite.

Sinigang

A tangy tamarind soup with vegetables and your choice of meat or shrimp.

Lechon

Roast pig, known for its crispy skin and tender meat, often served at celebrations.

Halo-Halo

A refreshing dessert made with crushed ice, sweet beans, fruits, and topped with leche flan or ube.
