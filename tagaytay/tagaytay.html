Tagaytay City

About Tagaytay

Tagaytay is a popular holiday town south of Manila on the Philippine island Luzon. Known for its mild climate, it sits on a ridge above Taal Volcano Island, an active surrounded by Taal Lake. Overlooking the area, People's Park in the Sky occupies the grounds of a never-finished presidential mansion. Picnic Grove is a recreation are with trails and a zip line.

Fun Facts

  • Legendarily, the word Tagaytay came from "TAGA" meaning to cut and "ITAY" which means father.
  • It is now classified as a Second Class City as declared by the Department of Finance.
  • Although Taal Volcano is readily seen from the ridge of Tagaytay City, it is actually in a different province!

Transportation

Jeepney

The jeepney is a staple mode of transportation in the Philippines, and it's a great way to travel around Tagaytay City without breaking the bank.

E-Trike

Tagatay is pulling out their E-Trike, because it’s a battery-based vehicle and it doesn’t produce smoke. It has wider space than an ordinary tricycle, and also has comfortable foam-coated seats.

  • By Private Car: The fastest way to reach Tagaytay is by driving from Manila via the South Luzon Expressway (SLEX) and Santa Rosa–Tagaytay Road, taking about 1.5 to 2 hours.
  • By Bus or Van: Buses and vans bound for Tagaytay are available from Pasay, Cubao, or Buendia terminals. They usually stop at popular areas like Olivarez Plaza or Tagaytay Rotonda.
  • Getting Around: Within the city, tourists can ride tricycles, jeeps, or taxis to reach attractions, restaurants, and scenic viewpoints.

Tourism and Development

    Tagaytay is a top tourist destination known for its scenic views, natural attractions, and family-friendly activities. Development in the city focuses on promoting sustainable tourism while preserving its cool climate and greenery.

  • Popular attractions include Taal Lake and Volcano, Tagaytay Picnic Grove, and Museo Orlina.
  • New hotels, cafes, and eco-friendly resorts continue to boost local tourism.
  • Efforts are made to maintain cleanliness and manage traffic from the growing number of visitors.

Culture

  • Locals are known for their warm hospitality and peaceful way of life.
  • They value family, faith, and community, often reflected in local celebrations and traditions.
  • Tagaytay’s culture blends modern living with a love for nature and clean surroundings.

Climate

  • Tropical climate with two distinct season.
  • Best time to visit: During the summer months from March to May, the temperature rarely exceeds 30°C (86°F), making it a comfortable escape from the heat of the lowlands. The coolest time of year is typically in February, with an average temperature of 18.8°C (65.8°F).

Food

Popular Filipino Dishes

Bulalo

A Tagaytay specialty — a hearty beef marrow soup simmered for hours with corn, cabbage, and potatoes. Perfect for the city’s cool weather.

Coconut Cream Pie

A chilled twist on the classic buko pie — made with a cookie crust, creamy coconut filling, and fluffy whipped cream from Puzzle Mansion in Tagaytay.

Crispy Tawilis

A local delicacy made from small freshwater sardines found only in Taal Lake. Deep-fried to golden perfection and best enjoyed with vinegar dip.

Things To Do

Top Activities in Tagaytay

Enjoy the View of Taal Lake and Volcano

Visit popular spots like Sky Ranch or People’s Park in the Sky for panoramic views of the Taal Volcano and its serene lake.

Have Fun at Sky Ranch

Ride the Sky Eye Ferris wheel, enjoy the amusement rides, and take pictures of the scenic Tagaytay landscape.

Go Horseback Riding at Tagaytay Picnic Grove

Enjoy a relaxing horseback ride while admiring the cool breeze and stunning views of Taal Lake. A great outdoor activity for families and friends.

See the Museo Orlina

Discover the creative glass sculptures and art exhibits by renowned artist Ramon Orlina in this scenic, multi-level museum.
