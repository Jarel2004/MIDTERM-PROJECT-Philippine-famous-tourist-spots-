Manila City

About Manila City

Manila, the capital of the Philippines, is a vibrant metropolis blending Spanish colonial architecture with modern skyscrapers. It serves as the country’s political, cultural, and economic center, offering travelers a mix of history, cuisine, and lively urban life. Popular attractions include Intramuros, Rizal Park, and the National Museum Complex.

Fun Facts

  • Manila is one of the most densely populated cities in the world.
  • It is home to Intramuros, the historic “Walled City” built during the Spanish colonial period.
  • Jeepneys, one of Manila’s most iconic symbols, were originally made from surplus World War II jeeps.

Transportation

Jeepney

The most common and colorful form of public transport in Manila — cheap, iconic, and perfect for short city rides.

MRT/LRT

The city’s elevated train systems are a fast way to avoid traffic and travel between major business districts and shopping areas.

  • By Air: Manila is accessible via Ninoy Aquino International Airport (NAIA), which serves both domestic and international flights.
  • Local Transport: Around the city, you can take jeepneys, buses, taxis, or trains to explore different districts and landmarks.

Tourism and Development

    Manila continues to grow as a cultural and tourism hub. Restoration efforts are being made to preserve historic landmarks while new developments bring modern shopping malls, hotels, and entertainment centers. The government promotes sustainable urban planning to balance modernization with heritage preservation.

  • Key attractions include Intramuros, Rizal Park, and the National Museum of Fine Arts.
  • Modern spots like Bonifacio Global City and Mall of Asia attract both locals and tourists.
  • Waterfront projects and city clean-up campaigns are ongoing to improve Manila Bay’s condition.

Culture

  • Manila’s culture is a blend of Spanish, American, Chinese, and Filipino influences.
  • Locals are known for their resilience, hospitality, and strong sense of family and faith.
  • Festivals, street food, and music are integral parts of everyday life in the city.

Climate

  • Manila has a tropical climate with two main seasons — dry (November to May) and wet (June to October).
  • The hottest months are March to May, with temperatures reaching up to 35°C (95°F).
  • The best time to visit is during the cooler months of December to February.

Food

Popular Filipino Dishes in Manila

Filipino Street Food

Manila’s bustling streets are famous for affordable eats like fish balls, kwek-kwek (deep-fried quail eggs), isaw (grilled chicken intestines), and turon. A must-try for a true local experience!

Sisig

A sizzling Kapampangan dish made from chopped pork, onions, chili, and calamansi. Served on a hot plate and best enjoyed with rice or beer — a Filipino favorite in Manila bars.

Kare-Kare

A rich peanut stew made with oxtail, tripe, and vegetables, served with bagoong (shrimp paste) on the side. A comforting dish often enjoyed during family gatherings in Manila.

Halo-Halo

A colorful and refreshing dessert of shaved ice mixed with sweet beans, fruits, jelly, and leche flan — perfect for cooling down in Manila’s warm weather.

Things To Do

Top Activities in Manila

Explore Intramuros

Walk through the historic walled city of Intramuros, visit Fort Santiago, and learn about Manila’s Spanish colonial past.

Visit Rizal Park

Relax and take photos at Luneta Park, one of Manila’s most iconic landmarks dedicated to national hero Dr. José Rizal.

Food Trip in Binondo

Try authentic Chinese-Filipino dishes in Binondo, the world’s oldest Chinatown, known for its vibrant food scene and culture.

Watch the Sunset at Manila Bay

Enjoy the famous Manila Bay sunset along Roxas Boulevard, a romantic spot for locals and tourists alike.
